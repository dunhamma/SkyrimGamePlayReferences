# Crime Mechanics Notes

Crime is one of Skyrim's most useful design surfaces because it separates public order from private morality.

## Design Implications

1. **Reported crime is not all wrongdoing.** Bounty tracks witnessed or reported crime. A mod may care about hidden actions, but it needs separate curated signals for that.
2. **Hold scope matters.** Civic standing should be local where possible. A Whiterun bounty should not automatically mean every faction or system has judged the player.
3. **War and faction contexts matter.** Killing an enemy soldier in a battle, killing a bandit, murdering a civilian, and assassinating a quest target are not equivalent.
4. **Avoid double punishment.** If crime produces bounty, jail, confiscation, or hostile guards, a mod should add extra consequence only when it creates roleplay, not redundant pain.
5. **Use recovery.** Paying fines, serving jail time, confession, restitution, or faction-specific reparation can turn crime from a flat penalty into a story loop.
